Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 July 2026

This notice explains how ParamPure handles your personal data, and sets out your rights under India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act"). It is written to be read, not to be survived — if anything here is unclear, write to our Grievance Officer (section 11) and we will explain it.

1. Who is responsible for your data

ParamPure Supplies Pvt Ltd, Bengaluru, Karnataka, India, is the Data Fiduciary for the personal data described in this notice. Where you order from a seller on the platform, that seller separately handles the order details we pass to them in order to fulfil your order.

2. What we collect

  • Identity and contact — your name, mobile number, and (for business accounts) shop name, business address, and GSTIN where you choose to share it.
  • Order data — delivery addresses, order and invoice history, payment status, and credit terms where your account has them.
  • Delivery evidence — where an order is delivered, the delivery partner records a timestamp, GPS location, and a proof-of-delivery photo.
  • Device data — Firebase Cloud Messaging tokens so we can send order notifications, plus app version and crash diagnostics.
  • Usage events — the in-app actions needed to operate, secure, and improve the service.

We do not sell your personal data, and we do not use it to build advertising profiles.

3. Why we process it, and on what basis

Under the DPDP Act we process your data either with your consent or for a legitimate use permitted by the Act. Specifically:

  • To provide the service (consent) — authenticate you by OTP, place and fulfil orders, calculate pricing and credit, and issue GST invoices.
  • To keep you informed (consent) — transactional alerts about your orders, payments, and account.
  • Marketing messages (separate, optional consent) — only if you opt in. You can withdraw this at any time in Profile → Notifications without losing access to the service.
  • Legal and tax compliance (legitimate use) — retaining invoices and transaction records as required by Indian tax and commercial law.
  • Fraud prevention and security (legitimate use) — detecting abuse, and protecting accounts and payments.

4. Withdrawing consent

You can withdraw consent at any time — for marketing in Profile → Notifications, and for everything else by deleting your account (section 6) or writing to our Grievance Officer. Withdrawal is as easy as giving consent. It does not affect processing already carried out, and we may still retain the records that law requires us to keep.

5. Who we share it with

  • Sellers and delivery partners — the order details needed to pack and deliver your order.
  • Payment gateways (Razorpay) — regulated processors who handle payment instruments. We never store full card numbers.
  • Infrastructure providers — Google Firebase and Google Cloud host the service. Our data is stored in Google's asia-south1 (Mumbai) region; limited operational processing may occur outside India, subject to the DPDP Act's transfer rules.
  • Accounting — Zoho Books, where a seller connects it, for invoicing and GST filing.
  • Government authorities — only where legally required.

6. Deleting your account

You can delete your account and personal data from inside the app at Profile → Delete account & data, or from our public request page: Delete your account. Both routes use the same process, described in full on that page.

7. Retention

We keep account data for as long as your account is active. When you delete your account we erase your profile, addresses, notifications and other personal records, and we anonymise the buyer details on past orders. We retain the underlying invoice and transaction records — with your identity removed — for the period Indian tax and commercial law requires, typically eight years.

8. Your rights under the DPDP Act

  • Access — obtain a summary of the personal data we hold about you and how we process it.
  • Correction and completion — have inaccurate or incomplete data corrected or updated.
  • Erasure — have your personal data deleted, except where law requires us to retain it.
  • Grievance redressal — raise a complaint with us and receive a response (section 11).
  • Nomination — nominate another person to exercise your rights on your behalf in the event of your death or incapacity. Write to our Grievance Officer to register a nominee.

We respond to rights requests within 30 days.

9. Security

We protect your data with transport encryption (TLS), OTP-based authentication, role-based access control, server-enforced database rules, and encrypted backups with point-in-time recovery. No system is perfect. If a personal data breach occurs, we will notify the Data Protection Board of India and every affected user, as the DPDP Act requires. If you suspect a breach, tell us immediately at the address in section 11.

10. Children

ParamPure is a business-to-business service and is not offered to anyone under 18. We do not knowingly collect the personal data of children, and we do not undertake tracking, behavioural monitoring, or targeted advertising directed at children.

11. Grievance Officer

If you have a question, a rights request, or a complaint about how we handle your data, contact our Grievance Officer:

  • Email — niknikhilav@gmail.com
  • Post — Grievance Officer, ParamPure Supplies Pvt Ltd, Bengaluru, Karnataka, India

We acknowledge every grievance and aim to resolve it within 30 days. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may complain to the Data Protection Board of India.

12. Changes to this notice

We will notify you in the app when this notice changes materially, and update the date at the top. Continued use after the effective date constitutes acceptance.
